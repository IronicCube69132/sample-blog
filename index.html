Why 5 Seconds of Summer Is the Greatest Band of All Time

A breakdown of what makes 5 Seconds of Summer stand out — from evolution and songwriting to fan connection and long-term impact.

MusicOpinionEntertainment

When people argue about the “greatest band of all time,” the conversation usually turns into a mix of nostalgia, charts, and personal bias. But if you’re looking for a group that blends talent, authenticity, evolution, and a genuine connection with fans, it’s hard to look past 5 Seconds of Summer.

5SOS isn’t just another pop-rock band — they’re a generation-defining act that has proven, again and again, why they deserve the top spot.

Evolution Over Time

When 5SOS first broke out, they were known for energetic, guitar-driven tracks like “She Looks So Perfect.” But instead of staying in that lane, they evolved.

Their sound matured across albums, moving from pop-punk into polished pop-rock and eventually more experimental, emotionally rich music. Albums like Youngblood and Calm highlight a willingness to take risks and grow.

Songwriting and Authenticity

5SOS are deeply involved in writing their own music. Their lyrics explore real emotions such as heartbreak, insecurity, growth, and self-reflection.

Songs like “Ghost of You” and “Old Me” resonate because they feel honest and lived-in rather than formulaic.

Musicianship

  • Luke Hemmings – vocals
  • Michael Clifford – guitar
  • Calum Hood – bass
  • Ashton Irwin – drums

Each member contributes something distinct, creating a full, dynamic sound across both high-energy tracks and slower ballads.

Connection with Fans

From early YouTube covers to global tours, the band has built a strong connection with their audience.

Their openness and relatability make fans feel part of the journey, forming a community rather than just a fanbase.

Consistency and Impact

5SOS has maintained quality across their entire discography, continuously evolving while staying relevant.

They’ve also helped bring pop-rock back into the mainstream and influenced newer artists in the space.

Final Takeaway

Calling any band “the greatest” is always subjective. But when you consider their growth, talent, consistency, and fan connection, it’s clear why 5SOS is firmly in that conversation — and for many, at the very top.

What Petrol RON Ratings Actually Mean: Compression, Pressure, and Knocking

A deeper look at what 91, 95, and 98 RON actually mean inside an engine — and why pressure, temperature, and knocking are all connected.

AutomotiveEngineeringTechnology

At a glance, petrol grades like 91, 95, and 98 RON look like simple numbers on a fuel pump. But behind those numbers is a deeper piece of engine physics, connected to pressure, temperature, and how combustion behaves inside an engine.

This post explains what RON really measures, and why it exists.

Controlled Combustion vs Chaos

All petrol engines follow the same basic cycle:

  • Air and fuel are mixed inside a cylinder
  • The piston compresses the mixture
  • A spark plug ignites it
  • The resulting explosion pushes the piston down

In an ideal situation, that combustion happens exactly when the spark plug fires and spreads smoothly across the cylinder.

But sometimes combustion becomes unstable.

Where Knocking Comes From

As the piston compresses the air-fuel mixture, two things increase:

  • Pressure
  • Temperature

If the fuel cannot handle these conditions, parts of the mixture can ignite on their own before the spark event, or before the flame front reaches them.

This leads to multiple uncontrolled ignition points inside the cylinder. These create shockwaves that collide, producing sudden spikes in pressure. The result is the metallic sound known as knocking or pinging.

Knocking is not just noise. It is uncontrolled combustion, and it puts stress on engine components.

What RON Measures

RON stands for Research Octane Number. It measures how resistant a fuel is to knocking under compression.

The rating is determined by comparing the fuel to a reference mixture of two hydrocarbons:

  • Iso-octane (very resistant to knocking), rated at 100
  • n-heptane (knocks easily), rated at 0

A fuel rated at 95 RON behaves like a mixture that is 95 percent iso-octane and 5 percent heptane in terms of knock resistance.

Higher RON means the fuel can withstand higher pressure and temperature before auto-igniting.

Compression Ratio and Why It Matters

The compression ratio of an engine describes how much the air-fuel mixture is squeezed before ignition.

For example:

  • A compression ratio of 10:1 means the mixture is compressed into one tenth of its original volume
  • A ratio of 12:1 compresses it even further

Higher compression has benefits:

  • Greater thermal efficiency
  • More power extracted from fuel

However, it also raises cylinder pressure and temperature significantly, increasing the risk of auto-ignition.

The Link Between RON and Compression

There is a direct relationship between compression and required fuel stability:

  • Higher compression leads to higher pressure and temperature
  • Higher pressure increases the likelihood of premature ignition
  • Preventing that requires fuel with higher knock resistance

In simple terms:

  • Lower compression engines can run stable on lower RON fuel
  • Higher compression engines require higher RON fuel to maintain controlled combustion

RON can be thought of as a measure of how much compression a fuel can tolerate before becoming unstable.

Flame Front vs Auto-Ignition

To understand knocking clearly, it helps to think about how combustion travels.

Normal combustion:

  • Spark plug fires
  • A single flame front moves smoothly across the cylinder
  • Pressure rises in a controlled way

Knocking:

  • Parts of the mixture ignite on their own under pressure
  • Multiple flame fronts form
  • Pressure spikes abruptly and unevenly

The difference is controlled ignition versus spontaneous ignition.

Why Higher RON Fuels Are More Stable

Higher RON fuels are formulated to resist breaking down under heat and pressure.

They:

  • Require higher temperatures to ignite without a spark
  • Remain stable longer during compression
  • Allow ignition timing to stay controlled by the spark plug

This does not mean they contain more energy. It means they release energy in a more controlled way under demanding conditions.

Modern Engines and Effective Compression

Modern engine designs make this relationship even more important.

Turbochargers increase the amount of air entering the cylinder. This effectively raises pressure and temperature, similar to increasing compression.

As a result, turbocharged engines operate under conditions where knocking is more likely, increasing the need for fuels that resist auto-ignition.

Modern engines also use sensors to detect knocking. When it is detected, the engine can adjust ignition timing to reduce pressure spikes. This maintains engine safety but can reduce efficiency and performance.

A Simple Way to Think About RON

Rather than thinking of RON as a measure of fuel quality, it is more useful to think of it as a measure of pressure tolerance.

  • Lower RON fuels ignite more easily under pressure
  • Higher RON fuels remain stable at higher pressures

The number reflects how the fuel behaves under compression, not how much power it inherently contains.

Final Takeaway

RON ratings exist because engines operate at the intersection of pressure, temperature, and controlled combustion.

Knocking happens when the fuel-air mixture ignites unpredictably under compression.

RON tells us how resistant a fuel is to that happening.

In essence, it answers one key question:

How much pressure and heat can this fuel handle before it stops behaving predictably?

Why Every Moment Could Be Improved with a Backflip

A completely serious argument for why adding a backflip to almost any situation instantly makes it better.

OpinionFunBackflips

There are many ways to improve a moment. You can add music, good conversation, better lighting, or a dramatic pause. But there is one universally underrated enhancement that transcends context:

A backflip.

Whether it is a casual interaction, a stressful situation, or a major life event, the addition of a well-timed backflip has the potential to elevate the experience immediately.

The Core Principle

A backflip introduces three key elements into any moment:

  • Surprise
  • Athleticism
  • Commitment to the bit

These combine to create an outcome that is more memorable, more energetic, and objectively more interesting than the original situation.

Everyday Scenarios

Consider the difference in the following:

  • Walking into a room versus backflipping into a room
  • Finishing your coffee versus finishing your coffee and immediately doing a backflip
  • Leaving a conversation normally versus exiting via backflip
  • Celebrating a small win quietly versus celebrating with a full airborne rotation

In each case, the backflip version has more impact. It adds drama. It adds confidence. It adds a level of commitment that ordinary movement simply cannot provide.

Social Benefits

Backflips are memorable. People remember moments that break expectation.

A well-placed backflip can:

  • Create instant energy
  • Make interactions more engaging
  • Transform ordinary situations into stories
  • Give a moment a clear before-and-after effect

This is why the backflip should not be seen only as a gymnastic movement. It should be seen as a narrative device.

Professional Applications

While traditionally underutilised in corporate settings, backflips have potential when applied strategically.

Imagine:

  • Celebrating the completion of a project with a backflip
  • Closing a presentation with a backflip
  • Improving team morale with a perfectly executed backflip
  • Turning a standard status update into an unforgettable milestone

Results may vary, and workplace safety should always come first, but the impact is undeniable.

Risk Considerations

It is important to acknowledge that not all environments are equally suitable for spontaneous backflips.

Factors to consider include:

  • Surface stability
  • Available space
  • Physical capability
  • Proximity to furniture, pets, colleagues, lamps, or any object that does not deserve to be kicked

Where a physical backflip is not feasible, a conceptual or metaphorical “backflip energy” may still be applied. This means bringing boldness, surprise, and momentum to the moment without necessarily leaving the ground.

The Deeper Philosophy

At its core, the backflip represents a refusal to let ordinary moments remain ordinary. It is a small act of chaos, precision, and confidence rolled into one movement.

It says: this moment could have been normal, but we chose something better.

Final Takeaway

Not every moment requires improvement. But when it does, the question is simple:

Would this be better with a backflip?

In many cases, the answer is clearly yes.

NRL and State of Origin: How It All Started

How Australian rugby league grew from early interstate rivalry into the modern NRL — and why State of Origin changed everything.

NRLSportHistoryAustralia

The National Rugby League and State of Origin are closely connected, but they are not the same thing. The NRL is the week-to-week club competition. State of Origin is the representative rivalry where New South Wales and Queensland meet in one of rugby league’s most intense contests.

To understand why Origin matters so much, you need to go back to the early structure of Australian rugby league and the problem that made the old interstate format feel unfair.

The Early Rugby League Era

Rugby league in New South Wales goes back to the early twentieth century. The NSWRL history records that the code broke away from rugby union in 1907 and that 1908 saw the birth of the club premiership, interstate football and Test matches.

For decades, New South Wales and Queensland played interstate football, but the balance was often tilted toward New South Wales. Sydney clubs were powerful, and many talented Queensland players moved south to play in the stronger Sydney competition.

The Problem with the Old System

Before State of Origin, players were generally selected based on where they were playing, not where they had come from. That meant Queensland-born players who were playing club football in Sydney could end up representing New South Wales.

The result was a strange sporting tension: Queensland was often competing against players who were originally Queenslanders. The rivalry existed, but the selection rules weakened the emotional logic of the contest.

The Birth of State of Origin

In 1980, the concept changed. Instead of selecting players based on their current club location, State of Origin selection was based on where players were originally from.

The NSWRL’s Origin history explains that interstate rugby league had lost relevance in the late 1970s, and State of Origin was conceived so players could be selected on the state they were initially from rather than their current state of residence.

The first State of Origin match was played in 1980, and Queensland proved the idea worked by immediately becoming more competitive. The new format gave the rivalry its emotional centre: state pride, not just club geography.

Why It Took Off

State of Origin worked because it solved the core problem. Queensland could select Queenslanders. New South Wales could select New South Welshmen. The rivalry suddenly felt authentic.

That authenticity changed the energy of the games. Origin became less like an exhibition and more like a statement of identity. The match was not just about skill; it was about place, history, pride and belonging.

Where the NRL Fits In

The modern NRL came later. NSWRL history records that the premiership expanded beyond Sydney, the Super League War erupted in 1995, and the 1998 peace settlement led to the formation of a new body, the NRL, to administer the main premiership.

So the simple distinction is this: the NRL is the professional club competition, while State of Origin is the representative series that sits above club loyalty for a few nights each year.

Why Origin Still Feels Different

Club football is about consistency across a season. State of Origin is about intensity, identity and pressure compressed into a short series.

That is why players, fans and commentators often treat Origin as something separate from regular rugby league. It takes the best players and puts them into a rivalry with a deeper emotional charge.

Final Takeaway

The NRL gives rugby league its weekly rhythm. State of Origin gives it one of its biggest emotional peaks.

Origin started because the old system did not properly reflect where players came from. By fixing that, rugby league created a rivalry that became bigger, sharper and more meaningful than almost anyone expected.
